Part 1: Algorithm Deep Dive

Put simply, the TikTok algorithm runs on two things: the percentage of completion of your video, and the amount of watch time. Every time you post, your video enters a testing phase where it's shown to 200-300 people. TikTok analyzes how they react. If people watch for a long time, TikTok pushes it to more people (the traffic phase). If they scroll away quickly, it gets stuck in the '200-300 view prison'.


Likes and comments help, but mostly because when someone comments, the video is still looping in the background, jacking up your watch time!

Part 2: The Scroll-Stopper Gauntlet

The average person's attention span is tiny. You have THREE SECONDS to hook them. If the start of your video isn't engaging, they'll scroll right past. It doesn't matter how good the rest of your video is if no one sticks around to see it. Your job is to make that initial drop-off in viewers as small as possible.


Also, good lighting is crucial. People watch TikTok, they don't listen to it in a dark room. Make sure your video is visually appealing! You don't need a fancy setup, just turn on a light or face a window.

Part 3: The Consistency Crusher

Motivation is random, discipline is a superpower. You need to be posting at least once every single day. This builds momentum and teaches the algorithm about your content. For faster growth, aim for 3+ posts a day, but leave at least an hour gap between each one. Posting too much or too quickly can make TikTok think you're a spam bot and shadowban you. Don't post more than 5 times a day absolute max.


Myth busted: The exact time you post doesn't really matter. As long as you post during daylight hours when your audience is awake, the algorithm's testing phase will find viewers for you.

Part 4: The Discovery Engine

You need to give TikTok hints about where to push your content. Using hashtags like #fyp or #viral is counterintuitive. It pushes your content to EVERYONE, which hurts your watch time because most people won't be interested. Instead, niche down to the EXACT audience you want to reach. If you make GTA videos, use #gta, #gta5, #gaming. Use 3-5 relevant hashtags per video.


Your description is also vital. Use it to include keywords that describe your video, or use it to create curiosity (e.g., "The last hack changed my life") to keep people watching until the end.

Part 5: Troubleshooting

Are your views suddenly stuck at 0 or way below 200? You might be shadowbanned. This can happen if you violate TikTok's terms of service or if your activity looks spammy (like posting too fast or on multiple accounts at once).


How to fix it: Don't panic! First, slow down. Keep posting, but no more than 1-2 times a day with large gaps in between. Do this for a couple of weeks. You can also contact TikTok support. If nothing changes after a month, you might need to consider starting a new account. Don't be discouraged, you'll get there!
